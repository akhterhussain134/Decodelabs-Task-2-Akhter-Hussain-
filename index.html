Science & Tech

Research & Innovation

Cutting-edge programmes that redefine what technology can achieve beyond our atmosphere.

🤖

Artificial Intelligence

Neural networks that pilot craft, diagnose faults, and run simulations at machine speed — keeping missions safe when humans cannot intervene.

🦾

Robotics

Autonomous rovers and humanoid robots that explore environments hostile to biological life, from volcanic plains to frozen ocean floors.

⚛️

Quantum Computing

Quantum processors solve trajectory optimisation problems in seconds that would take classical computers millennia.

📡

Satellite Systems

A constellation of 500+ satellites forms an interplanetary communications mesh, delivering gigabit data from the outer solar system.

🌐

Deep Space Comms

Laser-based optical communication enables terabit-per-second links across hundreds of millions of kilometres of vacuum.

🌱

Space Agriculture

Closed-loop food systems that grow crops in microgravity — the biological engine behind long-duration crewed missions to Mars and beyond.

Space Facts

☀️

The Sun contains 99.8% of all the mass in our solar system.

🪐

A day on Venus is longer than a year on Venus.

🌌

The observable universe contains an estimated 2 trillion galaxies.

🚀

Light takes just 8 minutes 20 seconds to travel from the Sun to Earth.

❄️

Space is not a perfect vacuum — it contains about 5 atoms per cubic metre.

🌙

The Moon is moving away from Earth at about 3.8 cm per year.

Roadmap

Exploration Timeline

2025

Moon Research Program

Deployment of 12 research robots and the ground-breaking for Lunar Base Alpha's foundation ring.

2030

Mars Settlement

First six-person crew lands on Mars, establishing a pressurised habitat in the Jezero Crater region.

2035

Europa Mission

An ice-penetrating probe descends 30 km through Europa's crust to sample the subsurface ocean.

2040

Deep Space Station

Construction begins on a crewed waystation at the Sun-Earth L4 Lagrange point.

2050

Interstellar Exploration

Launch of the first laser-sail nano-probe towards Alpha Centauri — humanity's first step between stars.
